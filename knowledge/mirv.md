# MIRV — Multiple Independently-targetable Re-entry Vehicles

One missile carries several warheads, each steered to a **different target**. Introduced by the US Minuteman III (1970); now standard on heavy ICBMs (Sarmat: 10+, DF-41: up to 10) and demonstrated by India in 2024 (Agni-V, "Mission Divyastra").

Two effects:
1. **Force multiplication** — one launcher threatens many targets.
2. **Defense economics** — a defender must spend several interceptors per incoming missile, so attack stays cheaper than defense.

## Why it matters
MIRV is the reason missile counts understate arsenal size, and the core of why ballistic-missile defense at scale is an economics problem, not just a physics one.
